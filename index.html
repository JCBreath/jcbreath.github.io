About Me

Hi! I’m pursuing a Ph.D. in Computer Science at the University of Notre Dame, advised by Prof. Chaoli Wang and Prof. Ming Hu. My research focuses on machine learning, computer vision, graphics, and scientific visualization.

I previously completed my master’s and bachelor’s degrees in Computer Science at the University of California, Davis, working with Prof. Kwan-Liu Ma and Prof. Nelson Max. My work at that time focused on VR/AR and drone-based systems, particularly for immersive visualization and 3D localization.

I’m interested in developing intelligent visual systems that combine data-driven algorithms with intuitive and interactive user interfaces. My goal is to create tools that enable users to explore, interpret, and gain insights from complex, high-dimensional, or dynamic datasets more effectively. By integrating techniques from machine learning, computer vision, and graphics, I aim to make data analysis more accessible, efficient, and visually engaging.

VolSegGS: Segmentation and Tracking in Dynamic Volumetric Scenes via Deformable 3D Gaussians

VolSegGS is a lightweight, real-time framework for visualizing and analyzing dynamic volumetric data. Using deformable 3D Gaussians and advanced segmentation techniques, it enables interactive view synthesis, segmentation, and region tracking on low-end machines. VolSegGS empowers scientists to explore time-varying data efficiently, overcoming traditional computational and resource limitations.

ReVolVE: Neural Reconstruction of Volumes for Visualization Enhancement of Direct Volume Rendering

ReVolVE is a novel neural framework that reconstructs high-quality density and color volumes from unlit DVR images, enabling flexible visualization enhancements. Using compact volume representation and segmentation, it supports novel view synthesis and downstream rendering. ReVolVE outperforms existing methods across datasets, advancing scientific visualization with efficient, high-fidelity volume reconstruction.

Read this paper

ViSNeRF: Efficient Multidimensional Neural Radiance Field Representation for Visualization Synthesis of Dynamic Volumetric Scenes

ViSNeRF is a 3D-aware visualization synthesis framework that reconstructs dynamic volumetric scenes from sparse labeled images using neural radiance fields. Unlike existing methods, ViSNeRF requires fewer samples and less training time while enabling flexible exploration across visualization parameters. It achieves state-of-the-art performance, addressing storage and rendering limitations in scientific visualization.

Leveraging Zero-Shot Learning on Street-View Imagery for Built Environment Variable Analysis

This is a deep learning approach for analyzing built environment variables (BEVs) using Google Street View images. Combining open-set object detection with vision-language models, our method accurately identifies buildings and classifies wall materials without finetuning. This scalable, cost-effective solution supports architects in mitigating climate-related health risks in vulnerable communities.

NeSurf: A Comparative Study of Neural Surface Reconstruction for Scientific Visualization

This study compares neural surface reconstruction methods for scientific visualization using multi-view rendered images. Categorizing ten approaches into neural radiance fields and neural implicit surfaces, we highlight the advantages of distance functions for accurate, smooth reconstructions. NeuS2 excels with closed surfaces, while NeUDF shows promise for open surfaces. Benchmark dataset provided.

Read this paper

GMT: A Deep Learning Approach to Generalized Multivariate Translation for Scientific Data Analysis and Visualization

GMT is a deep learning framework for multivariate time-varying data translation in scientific visualization. Unlike V2V’s one-to-one translation, GMT supports one-to-many and many-to-many mappings using a StarGAN-based architecture. Through extensive comparisons and experiments, GMT demonstrates superior translation quality and generalization, advancing multivariate data analysis and visualization capabilities.
